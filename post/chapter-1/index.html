Impact of Selected International HRM Practices on Expatriates Job Performance

This project focuses on analyzing the impact of International Human Resource Practices (IHRP) on Expatriates’ Job Performance within ABC (Pvt) Ltd. The study examines three key factors of IHRP: international training and development, international performance appraisals, and international compensations. Through a sample of 60 respondents, the study utilizes SPSS version 22 for data analysis. The analysis encompasses reliability and validity testing of instruments, demographic profiling of expatriates, frequency distribution analysis for both IHRP and job performance, hypotheses testing, implications, recommendations, and limitations. Findings indicate a positive relationship between IHRP factors and expatriates’ job performance, with international compensation practices exhibiting the strongest correlation. The study suggests enhancing international compensation and training and development practices to improve expatriate job performance at ABC (Pvt) Ltd
