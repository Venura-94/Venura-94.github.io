Exploring Base Dimension Reduction PCA Technique for Going Forward in ML

March 12, 2024

Delving into the fascinating world of clustering projects, I found myself intrigued by Principal Component Analysis (PCA), prompting me to consider it as a subject for an upcoming blog. PCA, a linear method, aims to retain a dataset’s variance while reducing its dimensionality by transforming initial features into uncorrelated primary components. This technique proves invaluable in scenarios where datasets exhibit high dimensionality, making it challenging to see underlying patterns. By standardizing the dataset and following a stepwise approach, PCA facilitates the identification of principal components, which capture the highest variance in the data. Through visualization and analysis of cumulative explained variance, one can determine the optimal number of principal components tailored to the specific goals of the project. With a comprehensive understanding of PCA’s principles and practical implementation, the potential for insightful data analysis and informed decision-making is vast.
